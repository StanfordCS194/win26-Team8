# 💭 Second Thought

Second Thought is a platform designed to help people **reduce online impulse purchases by intentionally adding friction to the buying process**.

Many online impulse purchases happen in the moment—after seeing a product on TikTok, Instagram, or through an influencer advertisement. In those moments, buying is often just **one click away**. Second Thought introduces a small moment of pause to encourage reflection before a purchase is made.

Our mission is to help users **practice intentional, mindful consumption**, promoting both **financial responsibility** and **more sustainable shopping habits** by reducing fashion’s significant contribution to carbon emissions.

This project was developed for **Stanford CS194 (Winter 2026) Team 8**.

# ⚙️ How It Works

Second Thought consists of **two main components** that work together to intervene at the right moment.

## 1️⃣ Chrome Extension – Interrupt the Impulse

The Chrome extension activates when a user attempts to purchase an item online.

Instead of allowing an immediate purchase, the extension introduces **intentional friction** by prompting the user to:

- Pause and reflect on the purchase  
- Consider whether the item is necessary  
- Delay the purchase decision  

This brief interruption helps users **break the automatic impulse-buying cycle**.

---

## 2️⃣ Website Dashboard – Reflect on Spending Habits

- **Track saved items** along with user-defined constraints and reflections  
- **Explore multiple perspectives on purchases**, including timeline and goal-based views  
- **Identify spending patterns and macro consumption trends** through the user profile  

Together, the extension and dashboard create a **feedback loop between action and reflection**.

---

# 💡 Why This Matters

Consumers spend an average of **$5,400 per year on impulse purchases**.

Online shopping platforms are intentionally designed to minimize friction and maximize purchasing speed. Second Thought flips this dynamic by **reintroducing friction as a design tool for self-control**.

By helping users slow down and reflect, Second Thought aims to:

- Reduce unnecessary spending  
- Encourage intentional purchasing  
- Promote more sustainable consumption habits
